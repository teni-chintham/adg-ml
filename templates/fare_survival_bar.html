Fare vs Survival

This bar plot explores the relationship between Fare and Survival Rate on the Titanic.

What Does This Bar Plot Show?

The graph illustrates a non-linear increase in survival probability as fare amounts rise.
In general, passengers who paid higher fares had a greater chance of survival.

Key Takeaways:

By analyzing the Fare column, we can conclude:

  • Higher fare values are strongly associated with increased survival rates.
  • The relationship is non-linear, meaning survival does not increase at a constant rate with fare.
  • There is an outlier where a high-fare passenger did not survive, but this can be considered an exception.
  • Overall, paying more for a ticket significantly improved the likelihood of survival.
