SibSp vs. Survival Rate

This visualization helps us understand the relationship between the number of siblings/spouses aboard (SibSp) and survival rate.

What Does This Graph Show?

The bar chart represents the survival rate for different SibSp values.
The red trendline highlights the overall pattern, showing that having exactly one sibling/spouse led to the highest survival rate, while larger family sizes had lower survival chances.

Key Takeaways:

  • Passengers with one sibling/spouse had the highest survival rate.
  • Survival for zero and two siblings/spouses was similar.
  • Passengers with four or more siblings/spouses had the lowest survival rate.
  • The trendline suggests that both being alone and having too many family members reduced survival probability.
