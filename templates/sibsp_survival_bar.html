SibSp vs. Survival

This bar plot examines the relationship between the number of siblings/spouses aboard (SibSp) and survival rate on the Titanic.

What Does This Bar Plot Show?

The graph represents the average survival rate for different SibSp values.
The pattern suggests that having **exactly one** sibling/spouse resulted in the **highest survival rate**, while larger families (SibSp = 4+) had much lower survival probabilities.

Key Takeaways:

  • Passengers with one sibling/spouse had the **highest survival rate**.
  • Survival for zero siblings/spouses was similar to having **two**.
  • Passengers with **four or more siblings/spouses** had the **lowest survival rate**.
  • Having **too few or too many** family members onboard may have impacted survival decisions.
